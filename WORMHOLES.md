# Wormhole Intelligence & Identification

This guide covers the "holes in space" logic—statics, identification, and the tools needed to survive in J-space.

## 1. Statics 101

Every system in wormhole space (J-space) has at least one **Static** connection. 

*   **Permanent Anchor:** A static is a permanent feature of that system.
*   **Infinite Cycle:** If a static collapses (due to time or mass), a brand new wormhole of the same type instantly spawns elsewhere in your system.
*   **Guaranteed Exit:** You are never truly "trapped" in a wormhole; you always have a door to a specific type of space (High Sec, Null Sec, or another Wormhole Class).

### Static Distributions
*   **Class 1 (C1):** Typically have a High Sec or Low Sec static.
*   **Class 2 (C2):** Unique because they always have **two** statics (e.g., one to a K-space and one to another Wormhole class). High traffic logistics hubs.
*   **Class 3 (C3):** Very popular for solo dwellers; many feature a High Sec static.
*   **Class 4-6 (C4-C6):** Usually have statics leading to other high-class wormhole systems (e.g., a "Static C5").

## 2. Identification (Visual & Tooling)

### Visual "Aura" Guide
You can identify the destination before jumping by looking at the color of the wormhole's event horizon:
*   **Blue/Cyan:** High Sec
*   **Yellow/Orange:** Low Sec
*   **Red/Pink:** Null Sec
*   **Dark/Grey/Black:** Other Wormholes

### K162: The Warning Sign
If you find a signature named **K162**, it is an **incoming** connection. Someone on the other side opened the door to your system. Treat every K162 as a sign that you are being watched.

Always check the "weather" on Anoik.is. Effects change ship stats significantly:
*   **Wolf-Rayet:** Armor/Small Weapon buffs.
*   **Pulsar:** Shield buffs / Armor resists penalties.
*   **Magnetar:** Damage buffs / Tracking & Targeting penalties.
